Welcome to the Artistry of Jingdezhen Porcelain

Welcome to Timeless Treasures, where the exquisite craftsmanship of Jingdezhen porcelain comes to life. This virtual museum showcases three iconic artifacts from China's Qing Dynasty, offering an immersive experience through detailed 3D models. Delve into the history, artistry, and cultural significance of these masterpieces, and explore the intricate designs that have captivated collectors and art lovers for centuries.

Featured Artifacts

Artifact 1: Qing Dynasty Covered Baluster Vase

This covered baluster vase is part of a five-piece garniture from the Qing dynasty, dating from the Kangxi reign (1662-1722). It is crafted from white porcelain with cobalt pigment under a clear glaze. The vase features a painted scene of a court lady being entertained by dancers and musicians, with floral decorations on the neck and a domed cover adorned with ladies, flowers, and rocks.

Artifact 2: Qing Dynasty Vase

This Qing Dynasty vase, circa 1700, originates from Jingdezhen, Jiangxi Province, China. Featuring a tall cylindrical shape with a narrow neck and outflaring rim, the vase is adorned with a mountain and lake scene painted in cobalt pigment beneath a clear glaze.

Artifact 3: Qing Dynasty Beaker-Shaped Vase

This blue-and-white beaker-shaped vase, dating from the Qing Dynasty (1662–1722), is part of a five-piece garniture from Jingdezhen, Jiangxi Province. It is painted in underglaze cobalt blue, featuring court scenes on the neck and court ladies in a garden on the body.
